# Momentum

Momentum trading in day trading focuses on capturing rapid, strong price movements in stocks (or other assets like ETFs) that are accelerating in one direction, often fueled by catalysts such as earnings reports, news events, sector momentum, or unusual volume spikes. The goal is to ride the "wave" of momentum early and exit before it fades, typically holding positions from a few minutes to a few hours (intraday only—no overnight holds).

This strategy suits volatile markets and requires discipline, fast execution, and strict risk controls, as momentum can reverse sharply. It's high-risk/high-reward, with most success coming from the opening 1-2 hours of the trading session when volume and volatility peak.

### Detailed Workflow for Momentum Trading (Step-by-Step)

1. **Preparation (Pre-Market / Before Open)**  
   - Review the economic calendar, pre-market news, earnings releases, and sector movers to spot potential catalysts.  
   - Set up your trading platform with real-time Level 2 quotes, time & sales, charts (1-min, 5-min, and daily for context), and key indicators:  
     - Volume (relative to average).  
     - VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price).  
     - Moving averages (e.g., 9/20 EMA on intraday charts).  
     - RSI or MACD for overbought/oversold confirmation (optional).  
   - Define your risk rules in advance: Risk no more than 1% of total capital per trade; aim for at least 2:1 reward-to-risk ratio (e.g., risk $100 to make $200+).

2. **Scanning for Momentum Candidates (Ongoing, Especially 9:30–11:00 AM ET)**  
   - Use a stock scanner (e.g., Trade-Ideas, Finviz, Thinkorswim, or broker tools) with filters like:  
     - Price change: +5–20%+ from open or pre-market gap up.  
     - Relative volume: 2x–5x+ average (or absolute high volume).  
     - Float: Preferably low (<50–100 million shares) for bigger moves.  
     - Price: Above key levels (e.g., previous day high, VWAP).  
     - Catalyst: News, earnings, FDA approvals, etc. (manual check).  
   - Focus on "gappers" (stocks gapping up/down significantly) or stocks hitting new intraday highs with surging volume.  
   - Build a short watchlist (3–10 stocks) to monitor closely—avoid scanning overload.

3. **Identifying the Setup and Confirmation**  
   - Look for strong directional momentum: Price making higher highs/lows in an uptrend (or lower for shorts).  
   - Common intraday patterns signaling entry:  
     - **Bull Flag** or pullback to support (brief consolidation after sharp move, then resumption).  
     - **Flat Top Breakout** (price consolidates under resistance, then breaks on volume).  
     - **Break of recent high** (e.g., pre-market high or opening range high).  
     - Volume surge on the breakout candle.  
   - Confirmation signals:  
     - Price closes above recent high/resistance on strong volume.  
     - Tape shows aggressive buying (large green prints).  
     - Avoid if momentum looks exhausted (e.g., RSI >80, fading volume).

4. **Entry**  
   - Enter long (buy) on confirmation of upward momentum—ideally on a pullback or breakout candle.  
     - Example: Buy as price breaks and holds above the high of the consolidation candle with volume spike.  
   - Use limit orders for precision or market orders in fast moves (but be cautious of slippage).  
   - Position size: Calculate based on stop distance—e.g., if stop is $0.50 away and you risk 1% of $50,000 account ($500), buy up to 1,000 shares.  
   - Enter early in the move for best risk-reward; avoid chasing extended runs.

5. **Risk Management (Stop-Loss Placement)**  
   - Set a **tight stop-loss** immediately after entry:  
     - Below recent support, pullback low, or breakout level (e.g., $0.20–$0.50 below entry for stocks under $50).  
     - Use mental stops only if very experienced; hard stops preferred.  
   - Trail stops as price moves in your favor (e.g., move to breakeven after +1R, then trail using recent swing lows or VWAP).  
   - Never move stops wider—cut losses quickly to preserve capital.

6. **Monitoring and Exit**  
   - Monitor for continuation: Rising volume, higher highs, no heavy selling on tape.  
   - Exit when momentum fades:  
     - Price stalls, forms reversal patterns (e.g., shooting star, heavy volume rejection).  
     - Hits predefined profit target (e.g., 2:1 or next resistance level).  
     - Volume dries up or RSI hits extreme (e.g., >70–80).  
     - Time-based: Many exit by midday as volatility drops.  
   - Partial exits common: Sell 1/2 at first target (e.g., +$200 profit), let the rest run with trailing stop.  
   - Full exit if stop hit or reversal confirmed—don't hope for recovery.

7. **Post-Trade Review**  
   - Journal every trade: Screenshot chart, note entry/exit reasons, catalyst, P/L, what worked/failed.  
   - Analyze weekly: Win rate, average R:R, common mistakes (e.g., overtrading, ignoring volume).  
   - Practice in sim/paper trading first to refine without real risk.

### Tips for Effective Use
- Trade only high-probability setups—skip if no clear catalyst or volume confirmation to avoid false breakouts.  
- Combine with volume analysis: True momentum needs buyer conviction (surging buys).  
- Focus on liquid stocks (high average volume) to ensure tight spreads and easy exits.  
- Best during strong market trends; avoid choppy or low-volume days.  
- Psychology: Stay disciplined—momentum trading thrives on quick decisions but fails on emotion-driven holds.

Momentum trading demands screen time, speed, and emotional control. Start small, use a demo account, and remember: Most day traders lose money—success requires consistent practice and risk management over chasing "hot" moves. Always trade with money you can afford to lose.
